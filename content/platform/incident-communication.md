      question: "The technical response is underway. But customers are seeing errors and executives want updates. How do I handle that?"
      detail: "Incident management is the technical response. Incident communication is the parallel track: keeping users informed, updating stakeholders, managing the support queue, and protecting the engineering team from being interrupted by 'any update?' messages while they're trying to fix things."
  to:
    - id: "post-mortems"
      question: "Incident is over, communication is done. Now what?"
      detail: "The post-mortem closes the loop. It answers the communication questions too: what do we tell customers happened? What do we commit to preventing? The post-mortem is both an internal learning document and the basis for external communication about what went wrong and what you're doing about it."
